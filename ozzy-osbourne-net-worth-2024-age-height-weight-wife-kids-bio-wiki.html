Early Life

John Michael Osbourne was born on December 3, 1948, in Aston in Birmingham, England, United Kingdom. His father, John Thomas Osbourne, worked at the General Electric Company making tools, but he died in 1977, and his mother, Lillian Osbourne, worked for some time at a factory, but she died in 2001.

Ozzy Osbourne has five siblings. His two brothers are Tony Osbourne and Paul Osbourne, and his three sisters are Gillian Osbourne, Jean Osbourne, and Iris Osbourne. Ozzy Osbourne lived with his family in a small house in his hometown. He dropped out of school at the age of 15.

Career

Ozzy Osbourne mentioned that a song by The Beatles titled “She Loves You” was one of the things that inspired him to become a musician. By the end of 1967, he got invited by Geezer Butler to join his first band “Rare Breed” as their vocalist, they went on a couple of shows before breaking up. Ozzy Osbourne and Geezer Butler got back together and invited Tony Iommi and Bill Ward to form “Polka Tulk Blues,” which got renamed to “Earth” before “Black Sabbath.”

Black Sabbath’s first album was a self-titled album released in 1970. Their second album, “Paranoid,” was released in the same year, and it debuted at one on two charts, including the Dutch Albums Chart and the United Kingdom Albums chart. Ozzy Osbourne had left and rejoined because he was working on a solo project.

Ozzy Osbourne left Black Sabbath upon fighting with Geezer Butler and Tony Iommi. Ozzy’s solo project was a band called “Blizzard of Ozz.” His debut album with the band was a self-titled album released in 1980, followed by “Diary of a Madman.”
