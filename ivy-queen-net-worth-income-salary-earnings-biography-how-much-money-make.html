Martha Ivelisse Pesante Rodrguez (born March 4, 1972), better known by her stage name Ivy Queen, is a Puerto Rican-American singer, songwriter, record producer, and actress who was born in the United States of America. She was born in Aasco, Puerto Rico, but she and her family subsequently relocated to New York City, where she remained until she completed her public education, after which she returned to Aasco with her family. The young Queen relocated to San Juan when she was 18 years old. It was then that she met record producer DJ Negro, who assisted her in gaining performance opportunities with a band named “The Noise” at a local nightclub. Her debut song, “Somos Raperos, Pero No Delincuentes,” was performed there, and it was her first public appearance. As a result of their aggressive and overtly sexual lyrics, The Noise garnered a lot of attention. Queen embarked on a solo career in 1996, releasing her first studio album En Mi Imperio, which was soon picked up by Sony Discos and published in 199Later, she released The Original Rude Girl, her second studio album for the Sony label, which included the smash song “In the Zone.” 

She is now on tour in support of the album. Ivy Queen, on the other hand, did not become well-known until she signed with an indie record company and released her third studio album, Diva, in 200Gold and Platinum Record certifications have been given to Queen’s albums Diva, Flashback, and Sentimiento by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). Her seventh studio album, Drama Queen, was released in 2010 and included the hit song “La Vida es As,” which reached number ten on the Billboard Hot 100. Among the topics that Queen’s music often explores are female empowerment, adultery, and romantic relationships, and she is sometimes referred to as “the Queen of Reggaeton.” Queen has also dabbled in acting, has volunteered for a cancer research organisation, and has created a doll based on her likeness for sale. In February 2012, she made her stage debut in Eve Ensler’s piece “The Vaginal Monologues,” in which she received critical acclaim.
